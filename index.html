    Clustering Optical Water Types

    Erdem Karaköylü

    2017-07-19 12:43

    This post describes an attempt at reproducing a study of ocean color optical water types (OWT). Here, remote sensing reflectance (Rrs) was used to partition samples into one of several water types according to their apparent optical properties. Naturally, the assumption is that the data set at hand - over 2400 samples including latitude, longitude, 12 bands in the visible spectrum, and chlorophyll. The authors of the study referred to above used fuzzy c-means clustering (FCM). However, K-means clustering yields results that are pretty close to FCM. So that's what I'll use here.
